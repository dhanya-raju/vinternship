Five weeks ago, I opened an email that promised a new chapter: an invitation to join the Vinternship program in MERN Stack at IIT Ropar! 

As an MCA student with a good amount of programming experience, I have already worked on several projects and am continuing to grow by exploring advanced technologies. 

To me, this felt like another opportunity to build on what I had been doing.

But looking back on the last five weeks, I realized that this was much more than that.

I have developed not just technical skills, but also improved my learning, thinking, and problem-solving abilities.

This program, offered through the ViBe platform, provided me with an entirely different type of learning experience than what I'm used to.

While it might seem simple, this has had an incredibly powerful effect on me.

In the beginning, it felt slower.

But I soon realized that working without really understanding what I'm doing, I won't learn as well and will not have as much fun throughout the duration of my working life.

It encouraged me to pause, reflect, and truly understand before moving forward, and everything has changed for me as a result of that.

Hands-On Case Studies: The Real Game-Changer

When I moved into the case study phase of my education, my understanding of learning changed further.

Applying theoretical knowledge to real-world problems required more thought and became more significant than just learning it.

Some cases were easy to solve, while others didn’t respond at all.

In those situations, I learned how beneficial it was to take short breaks because I wanted to approach it from a different angle and return later.

I also discovered the value of being fully present with the material rather than just rushing through it.

The Power of Intentional and Consistent Learning

An important takeaway from this experience is that real learning takes time and intention.

Thinking about a task will provide more clarity; however, learning through skimming will not provide the same clarity.

From my perspective, this internship has been about shifting from being a passive consumer of information to an active participant in the learning process. This shift has made the entire experience more meaningful.

There is, however, still plenty to build, develop, and understand.

But the difference now is that I feel prepared to confront those challenges head-on, with a stronger sense of direction and a clearer perspective.

I would like to express my sincere gratitude to the mentors and the entire VLED Labs team for designing such a thoughtful and engaging learning experience. Their guidance and effort have made this journey truly meaningful.
