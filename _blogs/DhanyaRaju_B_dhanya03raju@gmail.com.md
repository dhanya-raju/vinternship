Five weeks ago, I opened an email that promised a new chapter: an invitation to join the Vinternship program in MERN Stack at IIT Ropar! As an MCA student with a good amount of programming experience, having created several projects and continuing on that level of growth with further technological exploration. To me, this was merely another opportunity to keep building on what I had been doing.

But looking back upon the last five weeks, I realized that this was much more than that. I had gained many additional skills in my learning, thinking, and problem-solving abilities over the course of this program.

This program, offered through the ViBe platform, provided me with an entirely different type of learning experience than what I'm used to as it did not just have me consume information passively.

While it might seem simple, this has had an incredibly powerful effect upon me. It may take much longer to complete my work, but if I am going to continue working without really understanding what I'm doing, I won't learn as well and will not have as much fun throughout the duration of my working life. 

Because of this, I had to be much more intentional in my approach to learning, pausing to reflect and process information before proceeding, and everything has changed for me as a result of that.

Hands-On Case Studies: The Real Game-Changer

When I moved into the case study phase of my education, the meaning of learning changed. Suddenly, the importance of applying theoretical knowledge to real world problems that required thought and thoughtfulness became more significant than just learning it.

Some cases were easy to solve, while others didn’t respond at all. During those situations, I learned how beneficial it was to take short breaks because I wanted to reapproach from a different angle and come back to them again.

I also discovered the value of being present with the material rather than just rushing through it.

The Power of Intentional and Consistent Learning

An important takeaway from this experience is that real learning takes time and is thoughtful in nature. Thinking about a task will provide more clarity; however, learning through skimming will not give any clarity at all.

From my perspective, this internship has been about shifting from being a passive consumer of information to an active participant in the learning process. This shift has made the entire experience more meaningful.

There is, however, still plenty to build, develop, and understand. But the difference now is that I feel prepared to confront those challenges head-on, with an improved sense of direction and a clearer perspective.

I would like to express my sincere gratitude to the mentors and the entire VLED Labs team for designing such a thoughtful and engaging learning experience. Their guidance and effort have made this journey truly meaningful.
